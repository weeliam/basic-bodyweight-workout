The Workout

What are bodyweight workouts

Bodyweight workouts are exercises that use your body’s own weight as resistance. These types of exercises can be done anywhere. They are a great way to get a full-body workout without having to invest in expensive gym equipment. Exercises are an important part of any workout routine. They provide a way for individuals to strengthen and tone their bodies, improve their cardiovascular health, and increase their overall fitness level. Exercises can be tailored to individual needs, so that each person can focus on the areas of their body that need the most attention. Aerobic exercises can help with weight loss, while strength training exercises can help with building muscle and toning the body. Stretching exercises are also important for helping to reduce muscle soreness and maintain flexibility. It is important to include a variety of exercises in any workout routine in order to get the most benefit.

Benefits of Bodyweight Workouts

Bodyweight workouts have many benefits. They are a great way to get in shape and stay healthy. They are also convenient and can be done anywhere, anytime. Bodyweight exercises can also be modified to suit any fitness level, from beginner to advanced.

Before starting, consult your doctor

Before starting any new exercise program, it is important to consult with your doctor. Once you get the green light, start off slow and gradually increase the intensity and difficulty of your workouts as you get stronger and fitter.

Warm up

Before any type of exercise, it’s important to warm up for at least 5-10 minutes. This allows time for the body to adjust to the intensity of the upcoming physical activity and helps to reduce the risk of injury. After warming up, it’s important to stretch in order to maintain flexibility.

Warm ups before exercise are essential for any type of physical activity. Doing warm up exercises helps to increase body temperature, blood flow and helps to prepare muscles for the upcoming exercise. Warm ups also help to reduce the risk of injury and improve performance.

Common warm up exercises include dynamic stretching, jogging, jumping jacks, mountain climbers and squats. These exercises help to increase flexibility and range of motion, reducing the risk of injury. Dynamic stretching is a great way to warm up as it helps to increase body temperature and prepare the muscles for exercise.

It’s important to take the time to warm up and stretch before any type of physical activity. Doing so will help to improve performance, reduce the risk of injury and maximize the benefits of the exercise.

Mobility exercises

Mobility exercises are movements that help to improve flexibility, range of motion, and overall joint health. Examples of mobility exercises as a warm up before training include dynamic stretching, foam rolling, and dynamic warm-up drills. Dynamic stretching involves actively moving joints and muscles through their full range of motion, while foam rolling is a form of self-myofascial release that helps to reduce tension and tightness in the muscles. Dynamic warm-up drills involve movements that mimic the activity you are about to perform and help to activate the muscles and increase blood flow to the area.

The Exercises

The basic bodyweight workout consists of several exercises that target different muscle groups in the body. These exercises do not require any equipment and can be done anywhere, making it a convenient workout for those who do not have access to a gym.

Some of the exercises included in the basic bodyweight workout are push-ups, squats, lunges, planks, and mountain climbers. Push-ups work the chest, shoulders, and triceps, while squats and lunges target the legs and glutes. Planks help strengthen the core muscles and mountain climbers provide a full-body workout that improves cardiovascular fitness.

Other exercises that are included to the basic bodyweight workout include burpees and bicycle crunches. These exercises increase the intensity of the workout and help burn more calories.

It is important to perform each exercise with proper form and technique to avoid injury and maximize the benefits of the workout. Beginners should start with fewer repetitions and gradually increase as they become comfortable with the exercises.

Tempo

Tempo for an exercise for strength is the speed at which you perform the exercise. Generally, exercises for strength are performed with a slower tempo, meaning that you would take longer to complete the movement. For example, if you are doing a squat, you would take 3-4 seconds to lower yourself down, pause for a second at the bottom, then take 2-3 seconds to come back up. This slower tempo helps to increase the time under tension, which helps to maximize the strength gains from the exercise.

Sample workouts

This section will provide sample bodyweight workouts that can be done at home. These workouts can be modified to suit any fitness level. If one of the exercise in the workout is to difficulty just change it for an easier exercise. On the other side of the spectrum if you find the exercise easy you can progress to a harder one.

Bodyweight workouts are a great way to get in shape without having to go to the gym or use any fancy equipment. All you need is your own bodyweight and a little bit of space to move around in.

To get started, plan to do a basic bodyweight workout three times a week, making sure you have at least a day of rest in between each workout. This will give your muscles time to recover and prevent injury.

Reps & Sets

Reps

Definition of Reps

Reps = the number of times you perform a given exercise

Sets

Definition of Sets

Sets = the number of cycles of reps you complete

Reps and sets are an important part of any bodyweight workout. Reps, or repetitions, refer to the number of times you perform a certain exercise. Sets refer to the number of times you perform a group of exercises. For example, if you are doing a bodyweight workout that includes 10 push-ups, you would do 10 reps of push-ups and that would be one set.

Reps and sets are important for bodyweight workouts because they help you build strength and endurance. By doing multiple sets of an exercise, you are able to increase your muscular endurance and build strength. Doing multiple reps of an exercise also helps you to increase your muscular endurance and build strength.

When doing bodyweight workouts, it is important to make sure that you are doing the right number of reps and sets for your fitness level. If you are a beginner, it is best to start with fewer reps and sets and gradually increase as you get stronger. If you are more advanced, you can increase the number of reps and sets to challenge yourself and help you reach your fitness goals.

Example reps and sets:

  • - Push-up: 3 sets of 10 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Diamond Push-ups: 3 sets of 8 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Sit up: 3 sets of 12 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Bicycle crunches: 3 sets of 12 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Plank: 3 sets of 30 secs, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Dorsal raise: 3 sets of 12 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Squats: 3 sets of 12 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Lunges: 3 sets of 12 reps (each leg), rest 30 seconds in between sets
  • - Burpees: 3 sets of 12 reps, rest 30 seconds in between sets

Timed Circuit

A timed circuit with a set split of 10 to 40 seconds work and 5 to 20 seconds rest between exercises with a 1 to 2 minutes rest between sets is a type of high-intensity interval training (HIIT). This type of workout involves alternating short bursts of intense exercise with short periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. The goal is to push the body to its maximum capacity for a short period of time. HIIT workouts are an effective way to burn fat and build muscle in a short amount of time.

Example timed circuit:

  • - Push-up (1 minute)
  • - Squat (1 minute)
  • - Situp (1 minute)
  • - Diamond push-up (1 minute)
  • - Bicycle crunches (1 minute)
  • - Lunges (1 minute)
  • - Dorsal raises (1 minute)
  • - Burpees (1 minute)
  • - Plank (1 minute)

Repeat for 3-5 rounds.

These are just a few examples of the different types of bodyweight workouts you can do. Remember to start slow and gradually increase the intensity as you get stronger. And don't forget to stretch before and after your workouts to prevent injury!

Cooldown

Exercising is a great way to stay fit and healthy, but it’s important to make sure that you’re giving your body enough time to rest and recover. Cooldown after exercise is an important part of the process, as it helps to bring your heart rate and breathing rate back to normal and prevent any soreness or injuries.

Cooldown typically involves five to ten minutes of stretching and light aerobic activity, such as walking or jogging. This can help to restore your body to its pre-exercise state and reduce lactic acid buildup. Stretching can also help to reduce tightness in the muscles, improve range of motion, and reduce the risk of injury.

Some people also like to practice deep breathing or meditation during their cooldown period, as these activities can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation. Additionally, drinking plenty of water and having a healthy snack can help to replenish the nutrients that have been lost during exercise.

Including cooldown in your exercise routine can be a great way to help your body recover and prevent any injuries. Taking the time to properly cool down after exercising can help to ensure that your body is able to perform at its best.

Cooldown stretches

Cooldown stretches after training are stretches that are done at the end of a workout to help the body recover from the physical exertion and to reduce the risk of injury. They are typically held for 20 - 30 seconds and focus on the muscles used during the workout. Examples of cooldown stretches include calf stretches, hamstring stretches, chest stretches, and shoulder stretches.

Summary

In summary if you're looking to get in shape but don't have access to a gym or equipment, a basic bodyweight workout is a great option. By simply using your own body weight as resistance, you can build strength and improve your overall fitness.

To get started, plan to do a basic bodyweight workout three times a week, making sure you have at least a day of rest in between each workout. This will give your muscles time to recover and prevent injury.

When designing your workout, focus on exercises that target multiple muscle groups at once. Some examples include push-ups, squats, lunges, planks, and burpees. These exercises will work your entire body and help you get the most out of your workout.

Start with a warm-up to get your heart rate up and your muscles ready for exercise. This could include jogging in place, jumping jacks, or even a few minutes of light stretching.

Next, move on to your main workout. Aim to do three sets of each exercise, with 10-12 reps per set. If you're just starting out, you may want to start with fewer reps and work your way up as you get stronger.

After your main workout, finish with a cool-down to help your body recover. This could include stretching, foam rolling, or even a few minutes of light yoga.

Remember to listen to your body and take breaks as needed. If an exercise feels too challenging, modify it or take a break. And don't forget to stay hydrated throughout your workout.

By doing a basic bodyweight workout three times a week, you can build strength, improve your fitness, and feel great. With a little consistency and dedication, you'll be on your way to a healthier, happier you.
